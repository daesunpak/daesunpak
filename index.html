Understanding How to Use Real Estate Videos

The secret that most successful Realtors know is that the playing field is not level when it comes to Realtor.com, Zillow, Trulia and others listing sites.

For example, Realtor.com accepts Video Virtual Tours and places a "VR Tour" icon on top of the first image on the listing's webpage. Zillow and Trulia, do not accept Video Virtual Tours and Realtor.com does not provide an icon indicating that the listing has a walkthrough video or other professional video. As a matter of fact, Realtor.com does not even show walkthrough or professional videos on their website.

However, savvy Realtors know that placing their videos along side their Video Virtual Tours on the VR Tour link solves that problem. Snap2Close is the only company that provides that solution in the greater Phoenix area.

Conversely, Zillow and Trulia do not allow Realtors to use virtual tours on their websites, yet they will display an icon for a walkthroughs video or other professional video. Zillow displays an  icon on the first photo and Trulia simply displays a  icon on listings with a video.

Zillow, which also owns Trulia, allow Realtors and Zillow Certified Photographers to record a 2 minute video using their Zillow Mobile App. While these videos are proven to create many more listing clicks (potential home buyers clicking on the listing) they can also be very boring because they do not include any background music, graphics, titles, or professional editing.

To better learn about the typical quality of a Zillow Walkthrough Video then simply visit their website at Zillow.com and look at several of their videos. Most of these are done by Realtors who do not use any type of stabilization equipmemt and the resulting videos can be pretty jerky and difficult to watch.
